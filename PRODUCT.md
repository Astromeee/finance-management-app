# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Pocket Ledger is for students receiving pocket money, young people managing irregular income, and early-career salaried adults. They are usually checking their finances on a phone, often in short moments after spending or while deciding whether they can afford something. They need plain answers, not accounting terminology or a dense dashboard.

## Product Purpose

Pocket Ledger helps people plan from one income event to the next. It records the money they enter manually, protects bills and essential budgets, explains what is safe to spend today, and turns honest tracking into a calm daily habit. Success means a new user can understand their current position, record a transaction, and make a better spending decision in under a minute.

## Brand Personality

Calm, encouraging, satisfying, and trustworthy. The product speaks plainly, celebrates small progress, and never treats necessary spending or an interrupted habit as failure.

## Anti-references

- Dense financial control panels made from equal-weight statistic cards.
- Casino-like gamification, streak flames, coins, XP, leaderboards, or confetti for routine actions.
- Banking interfaces that imply Pocket Ledger connects to or moves money from real bank accounts.
- Shame-based warnings, moral judgments about purchases, or comparisons based on wealth.
- Decorative glassmorphism, neon glow, oversized radii, and motion without product meaning.

## Design Principles

1. **Answer today's question first.** Home should explain how the user's money journey is going today before showing history.
2. **Reveal complexity progressively.** Put calculations, reports, and setup detail behind deliberate actions rather than stacking them on Home.
3. **Make every insight explainable.** Safe-to-spend, affordability, and money-leak messages must expose the records and rules behind them.
4. **Reward participation, not wealth.** Celebrate tracking, planning, recovery, and goal progress without punishing unavoidable spending.
5. **Keep financial actions familiar.** Standard controls, clear confirmation, and reversible paths matter more than novelty.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Normal text must reach 4.5:1 contrast and large text and essential graphics 3:1. Essential meaning is never communicated through color alone. All flows support keyboard navigation, screen readers, 200% zoom, touch targets of at least 44px, reduced motion, and long names or PKR amounts without clipping. English copy remains simple and localization-ready for future Urdu support.
